D.4.4 mprimdec_lib

Library:

mprimdec.lib

Purpose:

procedures for primary decomposition of modules

Authors:

Alexander Dreyer, dreyer@mathematik.uni-kl.de; adreyer@web.de

Remark:

These procedures are implemented to be used in characteristic 0.
They also work in positive characteristic >> 0.
In small characteristic and for algebraic extensions, the procedures via Gianni, Trager, Zacharias may not terminate.

Procedures:

D.4.4.1 separator   computes a list of separators of prime ideals
D.4.4.2 PrimdecA   (not necessarily minimal) primary decomposition via Shimoyama/Yokoyama (suggested by Graebe)
D.4.4.3 PrimdecB   (not necessarily minimal) primary decomposition for pseudo-primary ideals
D.4.4.4 modDec   minimal primary decomposition via Shimoyama/Yokoyama (suggested by Graebe)
D.4.4.5 zeroMod   minimal zero-dimensional primary decomposition via Gianni, Trager and Zacharias
D.4.4.6 GTZmod   minimal primary decomposition via Gianni, Trager and Zacharias
D.4.4.7 dec1var   primary decomposition for one variable
D.4.4.8 annil   the annihilator of M/N in the basering
D.4.4.9 splitting   splitting to simpler modules
D.4.4.10 primTest   tests whether i is prime or homogeneous
D.4.4.11 preComp   enhanced Version of splitting
D.4.4.12 indSet   lists with varstrings of(in)dependent variables
D.4.4.13 GTZopt   a faster version of GTZmod
D.4.4.14 zeroOpt   a faster version of zeroMod
D.4.4.15 clrSBmod   extracts an minimal SB from a SB
D.4.4.16 minSatMod   minimal saturation of N w.r.t. I
D.4.4.17 specialModulesEqual   checks for equality of standard bases of modules if N1 is contained in N2 or vice versa
D.4.4.18 stdModulesEqual   checks for equality of standard bases
D.4.4.19 modulesEqual   checks for equality of modules
D.4.4.20 getData   extracts oldData and computes the remaining data
